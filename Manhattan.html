Explore Manhattan

Welcome to Manhattan. The Epicenter borough of NYC. It is the main cultural, financial, and media heart icon known for entertainment, business and art. It offers endless dining, activites and sightseeing spots. There is a major diversity in food and people as a result. Known landmarks include times sqaure, wall street and more.

Attractions: Must See Spots In Manhattan

View through the must see spots that you don't want to miss out on.

9/11 Memorial

The 9/11 Memorial is a memorial museum that makes part of the world trade center complex. It was created in memory of the victims of the 9/11 and 1993 world trade center killings. Location: 180 Greenwich St, New York, NY 10007.

Rockerfeller Center

The Rockerfeller Center is a complex of 19 buildings that spans across 48 and 51st in the midtown neighborhood. It showcases the 30 Rockerfeller Plaza, Radio City Music Hall, and more. These attractions are connected through underground passages filled with plazas, shops and art. Location: 45 Rockerfeller Plaza, New York, NY 10111.

American Museum of Natural History

The American Museum of Natural History is a natural history museum that showcases natural wonders of Dinosaurs to outer space and beyond. Location: 200 Central Park West, New York, NY 10024.

Grand Central Station

The Grand Central Station is a commuter rail terminal for long and short commuter trains. It features a stunning architecture of Beaux-Arts, Celestial Cieling, tiffany clock, and a variety of restaurants and stores. Location: 89 E 42nd St, New York, NY 10017.

Roosevelt Island Tramway

The Roosevelt Island Tramway is a aerial tramway that connects Roosvelt island to the upper east side of Manhattan. Here you can enjoy unqiue views of the east river skyline. Location: 254 E 60th St, New York, NY 10022.
